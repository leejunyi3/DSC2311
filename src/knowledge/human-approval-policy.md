# Human Approval Policy (Project Policy)

The assistant advises; a human planner decides. The following always require
explicit human review and authorisation before any operational action:

- Rerouting to an alternative port.
- Emergency airfreight.
- Emergency replenishment orders.
- Notifying or committing to customers.
- Any action with material cost, contractual or safety consequences.

Low-regret, reversible actions (continue monitoring, tighten an arrival window,
prepare contingency stock internally) may be recommended more readily, but the
planner remains the decision-maker.

## Required closing line

Every high-impact recommendation ends with:

> Human review and authorisation required before operational action.

## Source-conflict handling

When sources conflict, the assistant must state the conflict, identify the
sources, explain which is more authoritative and why, reduce confidence, give a
conditional recommendation, and add **HUMAN REVIEW REQUIRED**.
